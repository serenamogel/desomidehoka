Shandon Floyd: Investigation Underway in the Tragic Death of a Missing Transgender Woman

 Posted on January 1, 0001  |  2 minutes  |  277 words  |  Aldo Pusey

The Richland County Sheriff’s Department is currently probing the mysterious death of Shandon Floyd, a woman who was reported missing last week. Authorities discovered Floyd alone and unresponsive just before midnight on Tuesday, near the 10000 block of Two Notch Road in northeast Richland County. This article delves into the details surrounding Shandon’s disappearance and the ongoing investigation into the circumstances of her untimely demise.

Shandon Floyd Missing: Details of the Disappearance

Deputies shared that Shandon Floyd was reported missing by a friend on November 9. The friend reported that Floyd had borrowed his car at around 3 a.m. but had not returned by 7:15 p.m. the same evening. Both Floyd and her friend were from Florence and were staying at the Roadway Inn at 31 Berkshire Drive while in Columbia, according to an incident report filed by the sheriff’s office.

The responding deputy, in an attempt to contact Floyd, faced difficulty as all calls went straight to voicemail, adding a layer of concern to the unfolding situation.

Current Findings:

An initial examination of Floyd’s body has not revealed any immediate signs of foul play. However, to ascertain the cause of her untimely death, the Richland County Coroner’s Office will conduct a thorough autopsy. The details of the autopsy are anticipated to provide more clarity on the circumstances leading to Shandon Floyd’s demise.

Ongoing Investigation:

The case remains under active investigation, with authorities committed to uncovering the circumstances surrounding Shandon Floyd’s untimely demise. As the investigation progresses, the Richland County Sheriff’s Department will provide updates and additional information to the public. The community awaits answers, hoping for clarity regarding the events leading to Shandon’s tragic end.
